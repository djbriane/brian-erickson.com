summary: Notes from a stint adjacent to a very large live-service team — release trains, telemetry, and the cost of a bad default.
tags: [Notes, Games]
---

A live-service game runs on telemetry the way a city runs on water. You don't notice it until it stops, and when it stops, everything stops.

A few things I took away:

- Defaults are the product. The settings screen is a confession that you couldn't decide.
- Release trains beat heroics. The team I admired most shipped the most boring weekly cadence I've ever seen.
- The team that owns the dashboard owns the roadmap.
